### Latent Space — Mistral: Voxtral TTS, Forge, Leanstral, & what's next for Mistral 4

Mistral's Chief Scientist Guillaume Lample and audio research lead Pavan Kumar Reddy join Latent Space to discuss their latest releases:

**Voxtral TTS** — Mistral's first speech generation model (not just transcription). It's a 3B parameter model supporting 9 languages, built on their Ministral backbone. Key innovation: an autoregressive flow matching architecture with an in-house neural audio codec that converts audio into 12.5 Hz latent tokens (semantic + acoustic). Unlike competitors using depth transformers with K autoregressive steps, flow matching cuts inference to just 4-16 steps, enabling real-time streaming for voice agents. Quality matches top competitors at a fraction of the cost. Open weights released.

**Mistral Small** — A merged "omni" model combining previously separate capabilities (general instruction following, Devstral for coding, Magistral for math, Pixtral for vision) into one 6B active parameter model with 256K context. Guillaume explains their strategy: build specialized models with dedicated teams, then merge once mature. Future capabilities coming: more coding, reasoning, legal, finance, and CAD.

**Leanstral** — A formal math/proof model. Guillaume argues that current reasoning models only work on verifiable problems (like AIME where answers are numbers). For proofs and software verification, you need formal systems like Lean where correctness is binary (compiles = correct). This opens up software verification — currently a tiny market limited to aerospace — which could explode with coding agents.

**Forge** — Their enterprise deployment and fine-tuning platform. Mistral emphasizes that closed-source models waste companies' proprietary data collected over years. Fine-tuning on domain-specific data (trillions of tokens in some cases) can create 10x cheaper, on-premise solutions that outperform general models. They help customers deploy privately for sensitive data tiers and customize for rare languages, specific acoustic conditions, and enterprise voice personalization.

**On open source** — Guillaume reaffirms it's in Mistral's DNA. They want intelligence accessible to all, not locked behind closed doors. Technical reports for every release detail what worked and what didn't.

Latent Space 播客——Mistral 首席科学家 Guillaume Lample 和音频研究负责人 Pavan Kumar Reddy 讨论了最新发布：

**Voxtral TTS** —— Mistral 首个语音生成模型（不仅是转录）。这是一个 30 亿参数的模型，支持 9 种语言，基于 Ministral 骨干网络。关键创新：自回归流匹配架构，配合内部神经音频编解码器，将音频转换为 12.5 Hz 的潜在 token（语义+声学）。与使用深度变换器（K 步自回归）的竞争对手不同，流匹配将推理压缩到仅 4-16 步，实现语音 Agent 的实时流式传输。质量与顶级竞争对手相当，但成本仅为一小部分。已发布开放权重。

**Mistral Small** —— 一个合并的"全能"模型，将之前独立的能力（通用指令遵循、Devstral 编程、Magistral 数学、Pixtral 视觉）合并为一个 60 亿活跃参数、256K 上下文的模型。Guillaume 解释了他们的策略：用专门团队构建专业模型，成熟后再合并。未来能力：更多编程、推理、法律、金融和 CAD。

**Leanstral** —— 一个形式化数学/证明模型。Guillaume 认为当前推理模型只对可验证的问题有效（如 AIME，答案是数字）。对于证明和软件验证，需要像 Lean 这样的形式化系统，正确性是二元的（编译通过=正确）。这打开了软件验证市场——目前仅限于航空航天的小众市场——随着编程 Agent 的普及可能爆发。

**Forge** —— 他们的企业部署和微调平台。Mistral 强调闭源模型浪费了企业多年积累的专有数据。在领域特定数据（某些情况下数万亿 token）上微调，可以创建比通用模型性能更好、成本低 10 倍的本地部署方案。他们帮助客户为敏感数据层级进行私有部署，并针对罕见语言、特定声学条件和企业语音个性化进行定制。

**关于开源** —— Guillaume 重申开源是 Mistral 的 DNA。他们希望智能对所有人可及，而不是被锁在封闭的门后。每次发布的技术报告都会详细说明哪些有效、哪些无效。

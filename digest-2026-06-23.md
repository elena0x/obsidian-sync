### The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck

**OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries**

**The Takeaway:** Reinforcement learning — not just bigger models — is the paradigm that lets AI systems make genuine mathematical discoveries.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts explains how AI cracked the Erdos problems, a class of mathematical challenges that had resisted automated approaches. The key wasn't scale alone — it was reinforcement learning, which Roberts compares to learning by doing versus merely watching demonstrations. Pre-training gives models broad knowledge; RL gives them the ability to explore, fail, and improve.

Roberts also draws a sharp contrast between OpenAI's informal, human-readable approach to math proofs and DeepMind's formal Lean-based system. The informal route, he argues, is more flexible and aligned with how mathematicians actually think — while still producing verifiable results.

His broader point: intelligence is becoming customizable. The winners won't be those with the biggest models, but those who can turn intelligence into something purpose-built for their domain.

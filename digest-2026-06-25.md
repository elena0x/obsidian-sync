### The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck

**OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries**

**The Takeaway:** Reinforcement learning — learning by doing, not watching — is the paradigm shift that lets AI systems make genuine mathematical discoveries.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts explains how modern AI cracked the Erdos problems, a class of mathematical challenges that had stumped humans for decades. The key wasn't bigger models or more data — it was reinforcement learning, which Roberts likens to learning a sport by playing rather than watching demonstrations.

Roberts argues that intelligence is becoming customizable, not just scalable. The real power comes from combining pre-training (absorbing vast knowledge) with RL (learning through trial and error). This duo is what makes today's AI systems capable of genuine discovery, not just pattern matching.

He also contrasts approaches: OpenAI favors an informal, flexible style for math proofs, while DeepMind pursues formal verification through Lean — a structured, machine-checkable proof system. Both paths are valid, but they reflect different philosophies about how AI should reason.

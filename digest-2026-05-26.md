### Roblox Product Lead Peter Yang

Peter Yang shared five key takeaways from his conversation with a16z GP Anish Acharya about the future of work in an AI agent-first world:

1. **Coding will eat all knowledge work** — AI coding agents now handle the first 80% of tasks (writing docs, building slides, pulling analytics), leaving only manual polish for the last 20%.

4. **Personal agents will understand us deeply** — Yang recounts his OpenClaw reminding him: "You keep talking about your career and business. Just remember your kids are 7 and 4. They're going to grow up soon."
5. **Human ambition has no ceiling** — The economy's shape is changing, not shrinking. More one-person companies and small teams will emerge as big tech layoffs continue.

He also pushed back on measuring productivity by token usage, calling it "almost as dumb as measuring by lines of code written."

### Latent Space: Mistral — Voxtral TTS, Forge, Leanstral, & what's next for Mistral 4

**The Takeaway:** Mistral is betting on a modular, open-weights approach to AI — releasing specialized models for specific use cases (like their new 3B Voxtral TTS) rather than building one giant generalist model, while also pushing into formal mathematical reasoning with Leanstral.

Mistral's Chief Scientist Guillaume Lample and Audio Research Lead Pavan Kumar Reddy joined Latent Space to discuss three major releases: Voxtral TTS (text-to-speech), Mistral Small (a merged multi-capability model), and Leanstral (a formal reasoning model).

**Voxtral TTS** is a 3B parameter model built on their Ministral backbone. What makes it novel is the architecture: instead of using a depth transformer with K autoregressive steps (which adds latency), they developed an autoregressive flow matching approach with an in-house neural audio codec. The codec converts audio into 12.5 Hertz latent tokens with semantic and acoustic components. Pavan explained that flow matching works better than discrete token approaches because audio has more entropy — "even the same word with your own voice could be inflected in so many different ways." The model supports 9 languages and is designed for real-time streaming voice agents.

**Mistral Small** represents a new approach to model development at Mistral. Instead of separate teams building separate models for different capabilities (coding, vision, reasoning), they've merged everything into one sparse MoE with 6B active parameters and 256K context. Guillaume described it as "the first time we merged all of this into one." The next capabilities they're integrating include legal, finance, and computer-aided design — domains where customers need specialized performance but public benchmarks don't exist.

**Leanstral** is their entry into formal mathematical reasoning using the Lean proof assistant. Guillaume explained the motivation: current reasoning models work well on verifiable problems (like AIME math competitions where answers are numbers), but most real-world reasoning lacks an easy correctness check. Lean changes this — "if it compiles in Lean, it's correct." They see applications beyond math into software verification, an industry Guillaume believes will grow significantly as coding agents mature.

Throughout the conversation, both speakers emphasized Mistral's commitment to open weights. "We really don't want to be living in a world where the best models are only behind closed doors," Guillaume said. They also discussed their enterprise strategy: helping customers fine-tune models on proprietary data, deploy on-premise for privacy, and build domain-specific solutions that are "10x cheaper" than using closed-source APIs.

### Latent Space：Mistral — Voxtral TTS、Forge、Leanstral 及 Mistral 4 的下一步

**核心要点：** Mistral 押注于模块化的开源权重策略 — 为特定用例发布专门模型（如新的 3B Voxtral TTS），而非构建一个巨型通用模型，同时通过 Leanstral 进军形式化数学推理领域。

Mistral 首席科学家 Guillaume Lample 和音频研究负责人 Pavan Kumar Reddy 做客 Latent Space，讨论了三大发布：Voxtral TTS（文本转语音）、Mistral Small（融合多能力的合并模型）和 Leanstral（形式化推理模型）。

**Voxtral TTS** 是一个基于 Ministral 骨干网络的 3B 参数模型。其创新之处在于架构：他们没有使用深度 transformer 加 K 个自回归步骤（这会增加延迟），而是开发了一种自回归流匹配方法，配合自研的神经音频编解码器。该编解码器将音频转换为 12.5 Hz 的潜在 token，包含语义和声学组件。Pavan 解释说，流匹配比离散 token 方法效果更好，因为音频的熵更高 — "即使是你自己说同一个词，也可以用无数种不同的方式抑扬顿挫。"该模型支持 9 种语言，专为实时流式语音 agent 设计。

**Mistral Small** 代表了 Mistral 模型开发的新思路。不再是不同团队为不同能力（编码、视觉、推理）分别构建模型，而是将所有能力合并到一个稀疏 MoE 中，6B 活跃参数、256K 上下文。Guillaume 形容这是"我们第一次把所有东西合并到一起"。下一步要整合的能力包括法律、金融和计算机辅助设计 — 这些领域客户需要专门性能，但公开 benchmark 并不存在。

**Leanstral** 是他们使用 Lean 证明助手进入形式化数学推理的尝试。Guillaume 解释了动机：当前的推理模型在可验证问题上表现良好（如 AIME 数学竞赛，答案是数字），但大多数真实世界的推理缺乏简单的正确性检查。Lean 改变了这一点 — "如果它在 Lean 里能编译通过，它就是正确的。"他们看到的应用不止于数学，还包括软件验证 — Guillaume 认为随着 coding agent 的成熟，这个行业将大幅增长。

整个对话中，两位嘉宾都强调了 Mistral 对开源权重的承诺。"我们真的不想生活在一个最好的模型只藏在封闭门后的世界，"Guillaume 说。他们还讨论了企业战略：帮助客户用专有数据微调模型、本地部署保障隐私、以及构建"比闭源 API 便宜 10 倍"的领域专用解决方案。

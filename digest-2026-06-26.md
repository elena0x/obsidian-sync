## Podcasts

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck** — "OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries"

**The Takeaway**: Reinforcement learning — not just scale — is what finally enabled AI to make genuine mathematical discoveries.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts makes a compelling case that we've crossed a threshold where AI can now solve problems no human has solved before. His team's work on Erdős problems — long-standing mathematical challenges — demonstrates that modern AI systems aren't just pattern-matching; they're discovering.

The key, Roberts argues, is reinforcement learning. Pre-training gives models broad knowledge, but RL is what teaches them to reason, experiment, and improve through trial and error. It's the difference between learning by watching demonstrations versus learning by doing — and the "doing" part is where the breakthroughs happen.

Roberts also pushes back on the "bigger is always better" narrative. Intelligence, he suggests, is becoming customizable. The future isn't just about building larger models; it's about combining pre-training with RL in ways that let us shape what a system is good at. He contrasts OpenAI's more informal approach to math proofs with DeepMind's formal Lean-based verification — two different philosophies for ensuring AI-generated mathematics is actually correct.

The broader implication: if AI can now make discoveries in mathematics — one of humanity's most rigorous domains — the range of what it can contribute to is about to expand dramatically.

Peter Yang (Product at Roblox)

Peter Yang shared a conversation with a16z GP Anish Acharya about the future of work in an AI-agent-first world. Key takeaways: coding agents will eat all knowledge work (he now gets 80% of docs, slides, and analytics done through AI before manual polish); small 2-3 person teams with agent swarms will outperform bloated orgs; task-completion apps will shrink while entertainment apps endure; and personal agents will understand us deeply enough to give unexpected life advice. He also pushed back on measuring productivity by token usage, calling it "almost as dumb as measuring by lines of code written."
Roblox 产品经理 Peter Yang 分享了他与 a16z GP Anish Acharya 关于 AI Agent 时代工作未来的对话。核心观点：coding agent 将吞噬所有知识工作（他现在 80% 的文档、幻灯片和分析都通过 AI 完成，再人工润色）；2-3 人的小团队配合 agent swarm 将击败臃肿的大组织；完成任务型应用会萎缩，而娱乐类应用会留存；个人 agent 将深刻理解我们，甚至给出意想不到的人生建议。他还反驳了用 token 使用量衡量生产力的做法，称其"跟用代码行数衡量一样愚蠢"。

Aaron Levie (CEO at Box)

Levie argues that AI agents don't eliminate work — they move it up a layer of abstraction. The new work becomes: figuring out what to tell the agent, giving proper instructions, providing context, intervening when it goes off-task, reviewing output, and incorporating it into something else. "You are now the editor. A manager. A producer. The taste matters, understanding the craft matters, and the skills ultimately matter."
Box CEO Aaron Levie 认为 AI agent 并没有消除工作，而是将工作提升了一个抽象层级。新的工作变成了：想清楚要告诉 agent 做什么、给出正确指令、提供上下文、在 agent 偏离时干预、审查最终产出、并将其整合到其他事物中。"你现在成了编辑、管理者、制片人。品味很重要，理解你所从事的技艺很重要，技能最终也很重要。"

Peter Yang (Product at Roblox, AI Tutorial Creator)

Yang shared five key takeaways from his conversation with a16z GP Anish Acharya about the future of work in an AI agent-first world. He argues coding agents will eat all knowledge work, small teams with agent swarms will outperform large orgs, task-completion apps will shrink while entertainment apps persist, and personal agents will develop deep understanding of users. He also pushed back on measuring productivity by token usage, calling it "almost as dumb as measuring by lines of code written."
Yang 与 a16z GP Anish Acharya 讨论了 AI agent 优先世界中的工作未来，分享了五个关键观点：coding agent 将吞噬所有知识工作；配备 agent swarm 的小团队将超越大型组织；任务类应用会萎缩而娱乐类应用会留存；个人 agent 将深度理解用户。他还反驳了以 token 使用量衡量生产力的做法，称这"几乎和用代码行数衡量一样愚蠢"。

Aaron Levie (CEO @ Box)

Levie pushed back on the idea that AI agents eliminate work entirely. He argues that when agents do work for you, the work just moves up a layer of abstraction — now you're the editor, manager, and producer. Planning and review stages become more important, not less. "The work is still there, just different."
Levie 反驳了 AI agent 会完全消除工作的观点。他认为当 agent 替你完成工作时，工作只是上移了一层抽象 —— 你现在变成了编辑、经理和制作人。规划和审核阶段变得更重要，而不是更不重要。"工作还在，只是形式不同了。"

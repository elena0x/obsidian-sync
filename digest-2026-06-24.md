**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck: OpenAI's Dan Roberts — Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries**

**The Takeaway:** Reinforcement learning is the key paradigm that enables AI to make genuine mathematical discoveries, not just bigger models.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts explains how AI systems are now solving mathematical problems that have stumped humans for decades, particularly the Erdős problems. The breakthrough isn't about scale — it's about a fundamental shift in how these systems learn. Roberts compares reinforcement learning to learning by doing versus watching demonstrations. The combination of pre-training plus RL, he argues, is what makes modern AI systems so powerful.

Roberts also contrasts approaches: OpenAI takes an informal route to math proofs, while DeepMind pursues formal verification through Lean. The implication is clear — intelligence is becoming customizable rather than just about bigger models, echoing a theme we're hearing across the industry.

## PODCASTS

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries**

**The Takeaway:** Reinforcement learning — not just bigger models — is what finally enabled AI to solve real mathematical problems that humans couldn't.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts argues that the real breakthrough in AI isn't scale alone, but the shift to a "learning by doing" paradigm. He points to AI's recent success on Erdos problems — long-standing mathematical challenges — as proof that reinforcement learning (RL) can discover solutions that supervised learning never could.

Roberts frames the difference simply: pre-training is like watching thousands of chess games, while RL is like actually playing chess, getting feedback, and improving. The combination of the two — vast pre-training plus iterative RL — is what makes modern AI systems capable of genuine discovery rather than just pattern matching.

He also draws a sharp contrast between OpenAI's informal, human-readable approach to math proofs and DeepMind's formal Lean-based system. Roberts suggests that while formal verification has its place, the informal approach is more aligned with how mathematicians actually think and communicate — and may be more scalable for real research.

The broader implication: intelligence is becoming customizable. The companies that win won't necessarily be the ones with the biggest models, but the ones that can turn intelligence into something uniquely their own.

### Box CEO Aaron Levie

Box CEO Aaron Levie argues that the most interesting thing happening in AI isn't that one model is getting smarter — it's that intelligence is becoming increasingly customizable. The companies that win won't necessarily be the ones with the biggest models. They'll be the ones that turn intelligence into something uniquely their own. He also weighed in on AI regulation, noting that it's easy to say we need an FDA for AI, but the capabilities of AI models have near infinite permutations, making purely objective metrics universally applied before every model release very difficult. He advocates for primarily regulating the applied uses of AI, where the risk actually shows up. Levie also made a bold prediction: "Open source going to win big."

### The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries

**The Takeaway:** AI is no longer just about pattern matching — it's beginning to make genuine mathematical discoveries through reinforcement learning, fundamentally changing how we think about machine intelligence.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts discusses AI's breakthrough in solving mathematical problems, particularly the Erdos problems. He explains reinforcement learning as the key paradigm, comparing it to learning by doing versus watching demonstrations. Roberts argues that intelligence is becoming customizable rather than just about bigger models, and that the combination of pre-training plus RL is what makes modern AI systems so powerful. He also discusses the difference between OpenAI's informal approach to math proofs versus DeepMind's formal Lean-based approach.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oWOz2htozfI

**The Takeaway：** AI 不再仅仅是模式匹配——它正开始通过 reinforcement learning 做出真正的数学发现，从根本上改变了我们对机器智能的认知。

OpenAI 研究员 Dan Roberts 讨论了 AI 在解决数学问题上的突破，特别是 Erdos 问题。他将 reinforcement learning 解释为核心范式，将其比作"通过实践学习" versus "观看演示学习"。Roberts 认为智能正变得可定制化，而不仅仅是关于更大的模型，pre-training 加 RL 的组合才是现代 AI 系统如此强大的原因。他还讨论了 OpenAI 在数学证明上的非正式方法与 DeepMind 基于 Lean 的正式方法之间的区别。

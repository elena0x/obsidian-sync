## PODCASTS

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck** — "OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries"

The Takeaway: Reinforcement learning is the paradigm shift that lets AI systems make genuine mathematical discoveries, not just pattern-match from training data.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts explains how AI recently solved Erdős problems in mathematics — a breakthrough that signals a shift from demonstration-based learning to learning by doing. He compares the two approaches: watching someone solve a problem versus trying, failing, and iterating yourself. The latter is what reinforcement learning enables, and it's why modern AI systems can now venture into territory no human has mapped.

Roberts argues that intelligence is becoming customizable rather than merely scalable. The combination of pre-training plus RL is what makes today's systems so powerful. He also contrasts OpenAI's informal, proof-sketch approach with DeepMind's formal Lean-based verification — two different philosophies for ensuring AI-generated mathematics is correct.

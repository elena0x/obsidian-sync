## Podcasts

**The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck — OpenAI's Dan Roberts: Why AI Can Now Make Discoveries**

**The Takeaway:** Reinforcement learning, not just bigger models, is what enables AI to make genuine mathematical discoveries.

OpenAI researcher Dan Roberts sits down to explain why AI has suddenly started solving real mathematical problems — specifically the Erdős problems that have stumped humans for decades. The secret isn't scale alone. It's a shift in paradigm.

Roberts describes reinforcement learning as the difference between watching someone play piano and actually practicing yourself. Pre-training gives AI the sheet music. RL makes it play. This combination — vast pre-trained knowledge plus iterative, feedback-driven practice — is what produces systems capable of novel discovery rather than mere recall.

He contrasts OpenAI's informal approach to math proofs with DeepMind's formal Lean-based verification. The informal route, Roberts argues, mirrors how human mathematicians actually work: exploring, guessing, refining. The formal approach is more rigorous but slower and less exploratory. Both have merit, but the informal method may be what unlocks creative breakthroughs.

The broader implication: intelligence is becoming customizable. The winners won't be those with the biggest models, but those who can turn intelligence into something uniquely their own — tuned to specific domains, feedback loops, and goals.
